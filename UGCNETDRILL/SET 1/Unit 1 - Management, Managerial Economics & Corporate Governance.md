**Q.PA-01** Consider a demand curve which takes the form of a straight-line cutting both axes. Elasticity at the mid-point of the line would be:
1. 0
2. 1.0
3. 1.5
4. 2.0

*Correct Answer:* (2)
*Explanation:* The elasticity of demand on a linear demand curve is calculated using the formula: Lower Segment / Upper Segment. At the exact mid-point of the straight line, the length of the lower segment equals the upper segment, rendering a ratio of exactly 1.0.

**Q.PA-02** Price taker firms:
1. Advertise to increase the demand for their product.
2. Do not advertise, because most advertising is wasteful.
3. Do not advertise because they can sell as much as they want at the current price.
4. Who advertise will get more profits than those who do not.

*Correct Answer:* (3)
*Explanation:* Under Perfect Competition, individual firms are price takers facing a horizontal perfectly elastic demand curve. At the prevailing price, they can sell any volume of homogeneous output, removing any commercial justification for advertising.
